# Editorial & Cultural Inspiration

Sites with strong editorial design language, cultural depth, or content-first experiences. Reference when building food/beverage, artisan, cultural, or content-forward client sites.

---

## Milez

- **URL:** <https://milez.jp/>
- **What it is:** Japanese platform documenting traditional craftspeople and artisans — Buddhist sculptors, Ainu textile weavers (attus), and other craft practitioners. Bilingual (JP/EN), organized by numbered artisan profiles.
- **Style tags:** wabi-sabi, minimalist, typographic-heavy, Japanese-editorial, contemplative, avant-garde
- **Notable elements:**
  - Animated logo via frame-by-frame walk cycle using PNG sprite sheets (character walks then disappears)
  - Audio on/off toggle for ambient sound
  - Character-by-character text rendering — each letter/word is its own DOM element for fine-grained animation
  - Japanese numeral section markers (一 二 三 四 五 六 七 八) instead of numbers
  - No traditional navigation bar — full-screen immersive layout
  - Bilingual content presented as design element, not an afterthought
- **Best for:** Artisan brands, craft businesses, cultural organizations, any client with a strong story and identity worth experiencing slowly

## Veggie Taipei

- **URL:** <https://vegan.ning-h.com/>
- **What it is:** Personal guide to plant-based and vegetarian dining in Taipei, Taiwan. Includes an interactive "Craft Your Perfect Taipei Eats Note" generator with downloadable output.
- **Style tags:** playful, editorial, scrapbook, fresh, handcrafted, local
- **Notable elements:**
  - Sticker-style illustrated elements layered throughout — gives a physical scrapbook feel
  - Animated scroll counters (digit-roll number reveals)
  - Custom filterable generator tool built inline (dietary restrictions → personalized restaurant list → downloadable)
  - Mixed photography of food and city scenes, not just food
  - Collapsible dot-menu pattern for clean navigation
  - WordPress + Bricks Builder (proof of concept: handcrafted editorial is achievable on budget platforms)
- **Best for:** Restaurants, food blogs, local guides, any food/beverage client that wants personality over polish

## Sustainable Horizons

- **URL:** <https://sustainablehorizons.ai/>
- **What it is:** An AI-generated interactive editorial experience by Dow Jones exploring sustainability themes. A collaboration between generative AI and journalism — not a product site, but a premium branded content piece.
- **Style tags:** immersive, 3D-rendered, luminescent, teal-palette, iridescent, editorial, generative-AI-aesthetic, nature-tech-fusion
- **Notable elements:**
  - Full-screen 3D rendered hero: a floating island/orb with hyperrealistic iridescent flowers in teal, purple, and blue tones — clearly AI-generated imagery
  - Oversized display typography overlaid directly on the 3D scene — letters bleed behind the 3D object creating depth
  - Mint/teal background gradient that complements the render palette
  - "Click to Begin" as the sole entry point — no nav, no traditional UI, full immersion from frame one
  - Circular progress loader on entry (count-up animation before the hero reveals)
  - Tiny particle bubbles drifting from the floating island
  - Built by a generative AI + Dow Jones collaboration — intended as an editorial showcase
- **Best for:** Brand storytelling campaigns, sustainability-forward brands, any project where the experience IS the content — high-budget immersive launches, interactive editorials, or concept sites pushing 3D AI aesthetics

## Heidelberg Materials CCUS

- **URL:** <https://ccus.heidelbergmaterials.com/en/>
- **What it is:** Standalone microsite for Heidelberg Materials (one of the world's largest cement producers) showcasing their global Carbon Capture, Utilisation and Storage (CCUS) work. Flagship project: Brevik, Norway (live 21.05.25). Positions a heavy industrial company as a climate technology pioneer.
- **Style tags:** dark, cinematic, WebGL, immersive, editorial-documentary, industrial-precision, ambient-audio, scroll-driven, zone-based, green-palette
- **Notable elements:**
  - Loads like a cinematic experience — 90% progress bar preloads 3D WebGL assets before the page becomes interactive
  - "Enter without sound" button on entry — ambient audio is a designed element, not optional background noise
  - Navigation paradigm: "Scroll to explore six zones" — six content chapters, each anchored by a unique 3D rendered object
  - No traditional nav bar — the UI chrome is almost entirely hidden to preserve immersion
  - Each zone transitions via scroll, revealing its 3D object and sparse contextual copy
  - All icons are custom SVG, stroke-only, ultra-thin weight (no fills, no bounding boxes)
  - Brand color: deep forest green `#004E2B` — rare and distinctive for industrial sector
  - Closer to an interactive documentary than a corporate website
  - Built at campaign/microsite scale — not their main corporate site
- **Best for:** High-stakes brand campaigns, sustainability/ESG storytelling, industrial companies wanting to escape the corporate template, any project where the brief is "make people feel something about this" rather than "explain what we do"
