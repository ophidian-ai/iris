# Food & Beverage Inspiration

Sites from restaurants, bars, cafes, and food brands. Reference when building any food/beverage client site.

---

## The Wolf's Tailor

- **URL:** <https://www.thewolfstailor.com/>
- **What it is:** Two-Michelin-star fine dining restaurant in Denver, CO. Tasting menu-driven, chef-led kitchen with zero-waste and regenerative farming philosophy. Tuesday–Sunday evenings.
- **Style tags:** editorial, literary, hand-sketched-illustration, collage, parchment-aesthetic, artisanal, typographically-expressive, dark, brooding
- **Notable elements:**
  - Roman numerals (I, II, III) as section dividers — reinforces a book/manuscript structure
  - Hand-drawn sketches and illustrations integrated into the layout (not photography-dependent)
  - Aesop fable (wolf in sheep's clothing) used as narrative copy — storytelling as brand identity
  - Collage photography style rather than clean studio shots
  - Site formatted as a physical printed artifact (copyright notice, reproduction rights language)
  - Sparse nav: Reserve and Contact only — no typical restaurant nav patterns
  - Footnoted superscript references linking to sustainability practice explanations (mimics academic writing)
- **Best for:** Fine dining, chef-driven restaurants, artisanal food brands, any F&B client with a strong philosophy or story — especially when the brand is as important as the menu

## Siamais

- **URL:** <https://siamais.co.uk/>
- **What it is:** Thai restaurant and cocktail bar in Brindley Place, Birmingham, UK. A la carte, banqueting, bottomless brunch, venue hire.
- **Style tags:** upscale-casual, warm, moody, food-forward, dark-backgrounds, high-contrast-photography, elegant, approachable
- **Notable elements:**
  - Hero slider with full-bleed food and atmosphere photography
  - Bold section headlines with oversized typography pairing (large display + small subhead)
  - Prominent "Book" CTA in the navigation — conversion-first
  - Image-linked tile grid for featured offerings (Bottomless Brunch, Venue Hire, Pre-Show Menu)
  - Split-section layout alternating image/text blocks
  - Utility-forward footer with address, hours, phone
  - SEO copy woven naturally into body sections
  - WordPress + Bricks Builder
- **Best for:** Mid-to-upscale restaurants, bars, any F&B client offering multiple experiences (dine-in, events, brunch) that need clear visual separation between offerings
